A simple 5-step workflow for autonomous AI systems:

* **Find** — Identify the next task.
* **Execute** — Perform the task.
* **Verify** — Independently validate the result.
* **Memorize** — Store context, decisions, and outcomes.
* **Repeat** — Continue until the objective is complete.

## Why This Matters

Reliable AI agents are not built on better prompts alone — they're built on reliable loops. The F.E.V.M.R. framework provides structure to autonomous systems, ensuring they:

* **Don't drift** — Verification catches errors before they compound.
* **Learn from experience** — Memory systems enable continuous improvement.
* **Stay on track** — Clear task identification prevents scope creep.
* **Build confidence** — Each iteration validates the previous one.

## The Loop in Practice

### Find
Identify the next task from your queue, goal, or current state. This could be a user request, a detected issue, or the next step in a plan.

### Execute
Perform the task with the best available approach. Use your tools, knowledge, and experience to get it done.

### Verify
Independently validate the result. Don't assume success — run tests, check outputs, and confirm the task meets requirements.

### Memorize
Store what you learned. Document decisions, context, outcomes, and non-obvious patterns. This is your system's growing knowledge base.

### Repeat
Cycle back to Find. Each iteration builds on the last, creating compounding improvement.

## Key Insights

**Verification is not optional.** It's the checkpoint that prevents bad states from cascading. Autonomous systems without verification create debt, not value.

**Memory scales learning.** Without storing patterns and decisions, you repeat the same mistakes. Good memory systems let teams (or agents) learn from collective experience.

**Find beats Execute.** The quality of task identification often matters more than execution speed. A misidentified task, however well executed, wastes cycles.

**The loop is fractal.** You can apply F.E.V.M.R. at multiple scales — for individual tasks, feature releases, or organization-wide initiatives.

## Building Autonomous Systems

If you're building AI agents, automation workflows, or self-improving systems, F.E.V.M.R. is a foundation. It's not fancy — it's just discipline applied repeatedly.

Reliable automation isn't about smarter algorithms or bigger models. It's about building systems that:
- Know what they're supposed to do (Find)
- Do it well (Execute)
- Know when they failed (Verify)
- Learn from failure (Memorize)
- Keep going (Repeat)
